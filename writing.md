# Poetry

## The False Front's Burnout (2021)
And in time, I’d become content with all my misery. 
They tie me down, but treat my struggle like a mystery. 

Cherry floorboards creak as I vanish in the night. 
Why am I so weak?
Dear moon, make me feel alright. 

My expressions grow dull, my face has gone still.
I’ve run out of time, I’ve run out of will.
Twilight guides me as far as she can.
But these storm clouds hide her, ‘don’t let go of my hand’. 
The slow breeze caresses my cheek, the only one that cares.
I carried down the road as it played with my hair.
Large homes with hollow souls sit and sway as I pass by, but the faces in the window will never see me cry.
And when I made it to the freeway, I knew I must go back.
Turn my back toward freedom, and head into the black.
I know I will return, soon that road will take me away. 
But until then I stop here, I know that I must stay.

Cherry floorboards creak as I vanish in the night. 
Why am I so weak?
Dear moon, make me feel alright.
